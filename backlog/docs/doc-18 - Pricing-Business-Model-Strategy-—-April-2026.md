# Pricing & Business Model Strategy — April 2026

Pricing model, competitive pricing context, revenue projections, and planned features that affect monetization. Source doc: `docs/development/reports/competitive-analysis-2026-04.md`

---

## Pricing Model: 90-Day One-Time Pass

**Not a subscription.** Deliberately leans into the episodic nature of job searches rather than fighting churn.

A senior-level job search typically runs 8–16 weeks. 90 days covers the full arc: positioning → search → prep → interview → negotiation → done.

### Why Not Subscription

| Subscription Problem | How 90-Day Pass Solves It |
|---|---|
| "Am I still using this?" guilt | Clear scope — buy when searching, done when hired |
| Cancellation pressure during low-activity months | No cancellation to forget |
| Churn metrics drag down business story | Every purchase is a completed transaction |
| Friction from ongoing commitment | One-time decision, lower barrier |
| Need to manufacture engagement between searches | No engagement pressure — users return when they need it |

### 7-Day No-Questions-Asked Refund Policy

Reduces the purchase decision from "is this worth $X?" to "is this worth trying for a week?"

Benefits:
- De-risks purchase at higher price points
- Signals confidence in the product (increases perceived quality)
- Creates natural onboarding urgency — users engage immediately
- Expected refund rate: 3–8% (users buy during active search, component library investment creates switching cost)

## Current Pricing: $49 / 90 Days

### Why $49 Is Likely Underpriced

| What Facet Replaces | What People Pay Today |
|---|---:|
| Resume tailoring (Teal, Jobscan) | $87–150 / 90 days |
| Interview prep (Final Round AI) | $126 / 90 days |
| Mock interviews (Interviewing.io) | $225–500 / session |
| Career coaching (1 session) | $200–500 / session |
| Executive resume writer | $500–2,000 one-time |
| Salary negotiation coaching | $500–5,000 or % of increase |

A user buying Teal + Final Round AI for 3 months spends $213–528 for commodity-level tools.

### Price Signals Quality

A senior engineer making $250K who sees $49 thinks "resume template." A $99–149 price point says "professional career strategy tool." The 7-day refund policy makes higher prices feel safe.

### Pricing Tiers to Consider

| Tier | Price / 90 Days | Includes | Buyer |
|---|---:|---|---|
| **Core** | $79–99 | Resume assembly + multi-vector positioning + export | "I need a better resume" |
| **Pro** | $149–199 | Core + job search vectors + interview prep + recruiter cards | "I'm running a full job search" |
| **Complete** | $249–299 | Pro + comp negotiation + coaching features | "I want every advantage on a $200K+ move" |

Alternative: single $99–149 pass including everything, maximizing simplicity and word-of-mouth.

## Planned Features That Affect Pricing

### Comp Negotiation (Pipeline Capstone)

Closes the interview pipeline: positioning → search → prep → interview → **negotiation** → offer.

Impact:
- A $10K salary improvement (5% on $200K) = 67x–204x ROI on a $49–149 pass
- Creates a **measurable ROI moment** — user can see exactly what Facet was worth
- Strongest justification for premium pricing
- Most powerful testimonial/word-of-mouth driver
- Should surface the ROI number explicitly ("your negotiated increase: $15K / Facet cost: $149 / ROI: 100x")

### Career Coaching Features (Long-Term)

Skills gap tracking, market positioning over time, promotion readiness.

Impact:
- Natural evolution toward **annual career membership** ($199–299/yr)
- Creates value between job searches (addresses episodic usage)
- Retention mechanism for the career identity model
- The component library accumulates value over time — this monetizes that accumulation

## Key Metric: Repeat Purchase Rate

If 30%+ of users buy again within 2 years, Facet has a **career relationship**, not just a job search tool. This is the signal that:
- The career identity model is sticky
- Career coaching / annual membership will work
- Long-term retention is viable
- The data moat grows over time
